transparency · choice · control

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They help the website remember your actions and preferences (such as login, language, font size, and other display preferences) over a period of time, so you don’t have to keep re‑entering them whenever you come back to the site or browse from one page to another.

Cookies can be “persistent” or “session” cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them, while session cookies are deleted automatically when you close your browser.

2. How we use cookies

At Boop Squad, we use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential cookies: Necessary for the basic functioning of our website, such as enabling secure logins, remembering cart items, and maintaining session stability.
  • Performance & analytics cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our site (e.g., which pages are most popular, how long users stay, any errors encountered). We use this data to improve our website and services.
  • Functionality cookies: Remember your preferences and settings (e.g., language, region, font size) to provide a more personalised experience.
  • Targeting/advertising cookies: Used to deliver relevant advertisements and track the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns. These cookies may be set by third‑party partners (e.g., social media platforms, ad networks).
  • Social media cookies: Enable you to share content on social platforms and may track your interactions with embedded social widgets.
Our commitment: We never use cookies to collect sensitive personal information without your explicit consent. You are always in control of your cookie preferences.

5. Managing cookies in your browser

Here’s how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data → Choose your preferred option.
  • Mozilla Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data → Manage settings.
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage website data.
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage and delete cookies and site data.
  • Opera: Settings → Privacy & security → Cookies → Choose your preference.

For mobile devices, please refer to your device’s settings guide. Blocking all cookies may result in a degraded browsing experience.

6. Cookie consent dialog

We provide a transparent consent mechanism via the dialog box at the bottom of this page. You can:

  • Accept All: Consent to all cookies, including analytics and advertising.

Your choice will be stored for 12 months, after which you will be prompted again. You can also change your preference at any time by clearing your browser cookies or using the “Cookie Settings” link.

Appendix: Detailed cookie categories & examples

To provide even greater transparency, we have expanded our cookie classifications with real‑world examples and additional context.

Essential (strictly necessary) cookies

These cookies are crucial for the website to function properly. They enable core features such as security, network management, and accessibility. Without these cookies, the website would not work as intended. Examples include:

  • boop_session: Maintains your logged‑in status.
  • boop_cart: Stores your booking selections.
  • boop_csrf: Protects against cross‑site request forgery.
  • boop_cookie_consent: Remembers your consent choice so we don’t ask again each visit.

Performance & analytics cookies

These cookies collect anonymised data about how visitors use our website. We use this information to improve site performance, identify errors, and optimise user journeys. Key analytics cookies include:

  • _ga (Google Analytics): Differentiates unique users.
  • _gid (Google Analytics): Tracks user sessions.
  • _gat (Google Analytics): Throttles request rates.
  • boop_referrer: Tracks which external site referred you.

Functionality cookies

Functionality cookies remember your preferences and personalise your experience. They are not essential but enhance usability. Examples:

  • boop_preferences: Stores your language and theme choices.
  • boop_fontsize: Remembers your preferred font size.
  • boop_region: Suggests relevant local content.

Targeting / advertising cookies

These cookies are used to deliver relevant ads, limit ad repetition, and measure campaign effectiveness. They are often set by third‑party advertising networks. Examples include:

  • fb_pixel: Meta (Facebook) pixel for retargeting and conversion tracking.
  • _gcl_au: Google Ads conversion tracking.
  • IDE (DoubleClick): Google’s ad personalisation cookie.

Social media cookies

These cookies are set by social media platforms when you use their sharing buttons or embedded content. They may track your interactions across sites. Examples:

  • Instagram session cookies: For embedded Instagram feeds.
  • Twitter (X) cookies: For tweet buttons and timelines.
  • Facebook social plugins: For like and share functionality.

How long do cookies last?

Cookie durations vary based on their purpose:

  • Session cookies: Deleted when you close your browser (e.g., boop_session, boop_cart).
  • Persistent cookies: Remain for a set period (e.g., 24 hours, 30 days, 1 year, or 2 years). We use the minimum duration necessary for each purpose.
